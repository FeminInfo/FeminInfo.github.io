Periods

Fast Facts:
-Menstruation is the monthly shedding of the lining of the uterus
-Menstruation usually lasts 3-5 days (don’t worry: anywhere from 2-7 days is completely normal)
-The average age to get your period in the US is 8 to 12 years old
-Tampons and pads can be used to control bleeding


Health Risks:
-TSS (Toxic Shock Syndrome) is a bacterial disease often associated with tampon use.
-It is advisable to seek medical attention if:
    ~ Your period lasts for more than a week
    ~ You soak more than 1 pad/tampon in 1-2 hours
    ~ More than 3 months pass between periods
    ~ You bleed in between periods
    ~ You have unusual pain during your cycle
    ~ Your regular periods become irregular

Nutrition



Fast Facts:
-The key to a healthy diet is moderation (only eating as much as your body needs)
-Take your time while eating meals and try to eat smaller portions
-Always eat breakfast and try to eat lighter towards the end of the day
-Eat lots of colorful fruits and vegetables, as well as whole grains
-VARIETY is key
-Aim to reduce sugar and sodium, which may be linked to depression and diabetes
-Lead an active life and strive to exercise for 30 minutes a day (even a walk counts!)
-Teens need an average of 9-10 hours of sleep a night and adults need an average 7-8 hours of sleep a night
-Healthy eating promotes growth, keeps your heart safe, and makes you a happier person

Substance Awareness

Fast Alcohol Facts:
-Your weight determines how much water is in your body to dilute alcohol (in most cases, if you are lighter, it takes less alcohol to intoxicate you)
-Men often have more body weight to dilute alcohol. Women typically have 70% to 80% less of a stomach enzyme alcohol dehydrogenase, which aids in the metabolism of alcohol before it enters the bloodstream.
-Fluctuations in hormones (related to a woman’s menstrual cycle) will also affect one’s blood-alcohol content; one week before and one week after menstruating women are likely to stay intoxicated for longer periods of time.



Fast Drug Facts:
3 Main Categories of Drugs-
Stimulants: Stimulants are drugs that stimulate the brain and central nervous system, speeding up communication between the two. They usually increase alertness and physical activity. They include amphetamines, cocaine, crack and some inhalants like amyl or butyl nitrites.
Depressants: Depressants slow down the activity of the brain and nervous system, slowing down the communication between the two. For medical purposes they can calm nerves, relax muscles and useful for sleeping disorders such as insomnia.
Hallucinogens: Hallucinogens interfere with the brain and central nervous system in a way that results in radical distortions of a user’s perception of reality. Profound images, sounds and sensations will be experienced, but they will not actually exist. These are vivid hallucinations.

Most used drugs by teenagers:
Marijuana (depressant)
Prescription drugs (varied)
Ecstasy (stimulant)
Inhalants (depressants)
Cocaine and heroin (stimulants)

Sex

Fast Facts
-Sex is a physical and emotional experience
-All types of sex (vaginal, oral, and anal) can spread sexually transmitted diseases but only vaginal sex can get a woman pregnant
   ~According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, over 300,000 births occur
each year for females ages 15-19
-Safe sex requires a some method of contraception including male or female condoms or birth control


-In California sex consent laws (referred to as the “affirmative consent“) require the mutual presence of a “yes“ rather than the absence of a “no“
-In California the 2015 legal age of consent for sexual contact is 18
   ~Several exemptions exist to the legal age of California consent including the “close-in-age exemption“ also known as the “Romeo and Juliet law,“ which states that two minors close in age both under the California age of consent to engage in sex without fear of prosecution
-The National Sexual Assault Online Hotline is 800-656-4673; please do not hesitate to call if you or someone you know is in danger

Healthy Relationships

Fast Facts:
-The 7 ingredients to a healthy relationship include: emotional responsibility, kindness/compassion, warmth/affection, time together, learning through conflict, trust, mutual attraction
-Communication is essential to a healthy relationship
-Respect your partner and their wishes; their feelings have value
-Relationships that are not healthy are based upon power and control instead of equality and respect
-Humiliation, insults, harmful physical behavior, yelling, etc. all count as forms of abuse
-Breaking up is difficult but a face-to-face interaction is always advisable to avoid text or social media messages

Mental Health

Fast Facts:
1. Low self-esteem is a thinking disorder in which an individual views him/herself as inadequate, unlovable, and/or incompetent. Once formed, this negative view permeates every thought, producing faulty assumptions and ongoing self-defeating behavior.
2. Among high school students, 44% of girls and 15% of guys are attempting to lose weight.
3. About 20% of teens will experience depression before they reach adulthood.
4. Teen girls that have a negative view of themselves are 4 times more likely to take part in activities with boys that they've ended up regretting later.



5. The top wish among all teen girls is for their parents to communicate better with them. This includes frequent and more open conversations.
6. 7 in 10 girls believe that they are not good enough or do not measure up in some way, including their looks, performance in school and relationships with friends and family members.
7. A girl’s self-esteem is more strongly related to how she views her own body shape and body weight, than how much she actually weighs.
